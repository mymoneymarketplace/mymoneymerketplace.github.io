How to Choose the Right Card as a Pet Owner

1. Consider where you buy most of your pet supplies

If you order primarily from Chewy or Amazon, the Amazon Prime Rewards Visa at 5% is the clear winner. If you buy pet food at the grocery store during your weekly shopping trip, the Blue Cash Preferred at 6% on supermarkets earns more per dollar. If you shop at dedicated pet stores like PetSmart or Petco, the Citi Custom Cash can earn 5% automatically. Match your card to your shopping habits.

2. Plan for emergency vet costs

Emergency vet bills can reach $1,000-$5,000 or more without warning. Having a card with a 0% intro APR like the Discover it Cash Back provides a financial safety net for these situations. Even if you have pet insurance, you often need to pay the vet upfront and wait for reimbursement. A dedicated emergency card with available credit can prevent financial stress during a pet health crisis.

3. Factor in all pet-related expenses, not just food

Pet owners often focus on food costs but forget about grooming ($50-$100 per visit), boarding ($30-$75 per night), vet checkups ($200-$400 per year), pet insurance ($25-$50 per month), and supplies. A flat-rate card like the Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on ALL of these expenses without worrying about categories. If your pet spending is spread across many vendors, simplicity wins.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for vet bills?
The Wells Fargo Active Cash is the best card for vet bills because it earns 2% cash back on all veterinary expenses with no category restrictions. Vet bills can be unpredictable and expensive ($200-$5,000+), so a reliable 2% on every visit adds up. For emergency vet situations, the Discover it Cash Back offers a 0% intro APR that lets you pay off large vet bills over time without interest charges.
Can I earn 5% cash back on pet food?
Yes. The Amazon Prime Rewards Visa earns 5% on all Amazon and Chewy.com purchases, which includes pet food. The Blue Cash Preferred from Amex earns 6% on pet food purchased at U.S. supermarkets. The Citi Custom Cash automatically earns 5% on pet stores if that is your top monthly spending category. Each card covers pet food through a different channel.
What is the best card for Chewy orders?
The Amazon Prime Rewards Visa earns 5% on all Chewy.com orders since Chewy processes through the Amazon payment system. This makes it the highest-earning card for online pet supply purchases. If you already have Amazon Prime, there is no additional cost for the card. Without Prime, the Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on Chewy orders with no membership required.
Do any credit cards cover pet insurance?
No credit cards include pet insurance as a built-in benefit. However, you can earn cash back on pet insurance premium payments. The Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on monthly pet insurance payments. Some cards offer purchase protection that may cover items bought for your pet, but this does not extend to veterinary care or pet health coverage.
What is the best card for emergency vet visits?
The Discover it Cash Back is the best card for emergency vet financing because it offers a 0% intro APR on purchases, allowing you to pay off a large emergency vet bill over 15 months without interest. It also accepts applicants with fair credit (580+), making it accessible as a backup card specifically for pet emergencies. The first-year Cashback Match doubles any rewards earned on vet expenses.
Should I get a separate credit card for pet expenses?
A separate card for pet expenses can help you track spending and budget for your pets more effectively. It also provides a dedicated credit line for emergency vet visits. Consider a no-annual-fee card like the Wells Fargo Active Cash (2% on everything) or Discover it Cash Back (0% intro APR for emergencies) as a dedicated pet expense card.
What is the best card for pet grooming and boarding?
The Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on grooming and boarding services since these are general service purchases. The Citi Custom Cash may earn 5% if pet services become your top monthly spending category. Grooming and boarding typically do not fall into bonus categories on most cards, making flat-rate cards the most reliable earners for these expenses.
Can I earn rewards at PetSmart or Petco?
Yes. PetSmart and Petco purchases earn standard rewards on any credit card. The Citi Custom Cash can earn 5% at pet stores if that is your top monthly spending category. The Wells Fargo Active Cash earns a reliable 2% on all pet store purchases.
