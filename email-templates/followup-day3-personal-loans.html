Five hard inquiries later, his score dropped to 599. Every one of those lenders denied him, and the ones he tried the following week saw the inquiries and said no too.

The mistake was not that Marcus had bad credit. The mistake was applying with hard pulls to multiple lenders at once.

Soft pull vs. hard pull

Soft pull: lets you pre-qualify and see real rates. Zero impact to your credit score.

Hard pull: happens at formal application. Drops your score 3-8 points each. Visible to other lenders for two years.

Always start with soft-pull pre-qualification. You get the same information as a full application -- real rates, real amounts -- without touching your score.
