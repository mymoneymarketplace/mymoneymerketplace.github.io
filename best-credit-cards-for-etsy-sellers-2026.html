How to Choose the Right Card as an Etsy Seller

1. Decide between a business card and a personal card

If your Etsy shop is your primary source of income or generates consistent monthly revenue, a business credit card like the Ink Business Cash or Amex Blue Business Plus provides better expense tracking and cleaner tax separation. If Etsy is more of a side hobby generating under $600/year, a personal card like the Wells Fargo Active Cash is simpler and still earns 2% on all your supplies. You can always upgrade to a business card as your shop grows.

2. Prioritize no annual fee cards for seasonal income

Etsy income is highly seasonal for most sellers -- the holiday rush from October through December may account for half your annual revenue. A no-fee card ensures you are not paying $95-$375 per year during slow months when your shop generates minimal income. Every card on our list has either no annual fee or a fee that is easily offset by rewards, making them all suitable for the variable income reality of craft selling.

3. Consider where you buy your supplies

If you buy most shipping supplies at office stores like Staples or Office Depot, the Ink Business Cash earns 5% on those purchases. If your craft supplies come from many different sources -- local fabric shops, online bead wholesalers, hardware stores, Etsy itself -- a flat-rate card like the Amex Blue Business Plus (2x on everything) or Wells Fargo Active Cash (2% on everything) ensures you earn rewards regardless of where you shop.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for Etsy sellers?
The Ink Business Cash is the best overall credit card for Etsy sellers because it earns 5% on office supply stores (where many sellers buy shipping supplies), 5% on internet and phone service for managing their shop, and has no annual fee. For sellers who want a simple flat rate on all craft supplies, the Amex Blue Business Plus earns 2x on everything up to $50,000 per year with no annual fee.
Should Etsy sellers use a business or personal credit card?
If you earn regular income from Etsy, a business credit card is recommended even if your shop is a sole proprietorship. Business cards help separate personal and business expenses for cleaner tax filing, and you can apply with just your Social Security number. However, hobby sellers making under $600 per year may find a personal card like the Wells Fargo Active Cash sufficient.
What is the best card for craft supply purchases?
The Amex Blue Business Plus earns 2x Membership Rewards on all purchases including fabric stores, bead shops, hardware stores, and online craft suppliers up to $50,000 per year with no annual fee. Since craft supplies come from many different types of stores, a flat-rate card that works everywhere is often more practical than a category-specific card.
Can I deduct craft supplies as a business expense?
Yes, if your Etsy shop is operated as a business (not a hobby), craft supplies, shipping materials, photography equipment, Etsy listing fees, advertising costs, and home studio expenses are generally tax deductible. The IRS distinguishes between hobbies and businesses based on whether you have a profit motive. A business credit card creates a clear paper trail for all deductible expenses.
What is the best card for Etsy shipping supplies?
The Ink Business Cash earns 5% at office supply stores, which is where many sellers buy boxes, mailers, tape, and labels. For shipping postage purchased online through services like Pirate Ship or Stamps.com, the Ink Business Cash also earns 5% on internet purchases. The Ink Business Preferred earns 3x on shipping services directly.
How should Etsy sellers handle seasonal income with credit cards?
Many Etsy shops earn 50% or more of annual revenue during the holiday season (October through December). Avoid carrying a credit card balance into slower months when income drops. Build a cash reserve during peak season to cover material purchases during off-peak months. A no-annual-fee card ensures you are not paying a yearly cost during months when your shop may generate minimal revenue.
Is it worth paying an annual fee as an Etsy seller?
Most Etsy sellers should avoid annual fee cards unless they consistently spend more than $2,000 per month on business expenses. The Ink Business Cash and Capital One Spark Cash Select both earn competitive rewards with no annual fee. Only consider a fee card if the higher reward rate on your specific spending categories clearly generates more value than the fee costs.
What is the best card for new Etsy sellers building credit?
The Discover it Cash Back is the best option for new Etsy sellers with limited credit history. It accepts applicants with fair credit scores starting at 580, charges no annual fee, and the first-year Cashback Match effectively doubles all rewards. This makes it an excellent starter card while you build both your credit score and your Etsy business.
