Landscaping businesses run on fuel -- mowers, trimmers, blowers, trucks, and trailers all need gas, and it adds up fast when crews are running multiple jobs per day across town. On top of fuel costs, landscapers spend heavily on plants, mulch, fertilizer, equipment maintenance, and seasonal labor. Most landscaping companies are small LLCs or sole proprietorships with strongly seasonal revenue -- spring and summer bring peak demand while winter months can see revenue drop by 50% or more depending on your region. The right business credit card helps manage this cycle by earning rewards during busy months and minimizing costs during the off-season. How to Choose the Right Card as a Landscaper

1. Calculate your monthly fuel spend first

Fuel is typically the single largest variable expense for landscaping businesses. If your crews burn through $1,000 or more per month in gas for trucks, mowers, and equipment, the Costco Anywhere Visa earning 4% on gas should be your first card. Pair it with a business card for supply purchases and you will maximize rewards across your two biggest expense categories.

2. Use a no-fee card if revenue is under $100K

Smaller landscaping operations earning under $100,000 annually should avoid cards with annual fees. The Ink Business Cash charges $0 per year while still earning 5% on phone and internet (essential for scheduling apps and customer communication) and 2% on gas. Once your revenue grows, you can add a premium card like the Amex Business Gold for its 4x rewards on high-spend categories.

3. Keep business and personal spending separate

Use a dedicated business card for all company expenses -- fuel, supplies, equipment, subcontractor payments -- and a personal card for everything else. This separation simplifies tax preparation, makes it easier to track profitability by job, and protects your personal credit if the business has a slow year. The Ink Business Cash for business and Wells Fargo Active Cash for personal is an effective two-card strategy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for landscaping businesses?
The Ink Business Cash is the best card for landscaping businesses because it earns 5% on office supplies, internet, and phone services used for scheduling and customer communication, plus 2% on gas stations which is critical when fueling mowers, trucks, and trailers daily. The $0 annual fee is especially valuable for landscaping companies that see revenue drop significantly during winter months.
How do landscapers manage seasonal cash flow with credit cards?
Landscaping businesses should use a no-annual-fee business card during the off-season to minimize costs, while maximizing rewards during the busy spring and summer months when spending on plants, mulch, fuel, and labor is highest. The Amex Business Gold is ideal because its 4x rewards automatically apply to your top spending categories, shifting as your expenses change from winter equipment maintenance to peak-season supply runs.
What is the best fuel card for landscaping equipment and trucks?
The Costco Anywhere Visa earns 4% cash back at all gas stations, making it the best fuel card for landscaping businesses that spend heavily on gas for trucks, trailers, mowers, trimmers, and blowers. A landscaping company spending $1,000 or more monthly on fuel can earn over $480 per year in gas rewards alone. The card works at all gas stations, not just Costco pumps.
Should landscapers use business or personal credit cards?
Landscaping business owners should always use a business credit card for company expenses. Business cards keep personal and business spending separate for tax purposes, offer higher credit limits to cover seasonal inventory purchases, and provide expense management tools. Even sole proprietors with an LLC or DBA can qualify for business cards using their Social Security number on the application.
What is the best no-fee card for landscaping companies?
The Ink Business Cash is the best no-fee business card for landscaping companies, earning 5% on phone and internet services and 2% on gas. For landscapers who want simplicity, the Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on all purchases with no annual fee and no categories to track -- though it is a personal card without business expense features.
What cards work best for buying plants and landscaping supplies?
The Capital One Spark Cash Plus earns unlimited 2% on every purchase including nurseries, garden centers, mulch yards, and landscaping supply houses. There is no cap on earnings, so even months with $15,000 or more in supply purchases earn the full 2% back. For larger businesses, the Amex Business Gold can earn 4x if landscaping supplies fall into your top two spending categories.
Can new landscaping businesses get approved for credit cards?
Yes. You can apply for business credit cards as a sole proprietorship using your Social Security number, even if your landscaping business is brand new. The Ink Business Cash is the best starting point because it has no annual fee and does not require years of business history. Report your projected annual revenue on the application and focus on building business credit through consistent on-time payments.
What is the best card for landscaping equipment financing?
For large equipment purchases, the Amex Business Gold offers an extended payment option that lets you carry a balance on purchases over $100 for an additional fee, which can help with cash flow when buying a new mower or trailer. The Capital One Spark Cash Plus earns 2% on all purchases but requires payment in full each month. For financing flexibility, consider a business line of credit alongside your rewards card.
