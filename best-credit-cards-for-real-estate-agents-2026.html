How to Choose the Right Card as a Real Estate Agent

1. Identify your largest expense category

For most agents, the biggest monthly expense is advertising -- Zillow Premier Agent, social media ads, print marketing, and direct mail. If advertising dominates your budget, the Ink Business Preferred (3x on ads) is the clear winner. If client entertainment and travel between properties are your top costs, the Chase Sapphire Preferred (3x dining and travel) provides more value. Track one month of spending before choosing.

2. Account for commission-based income variability

Real estate income is cyclical -- you may close three deals in one month and none the next. Choose a card with manageable payments and avoid charge cards that require full payment each billing cycle unless your cash reserves are strong. The Wells Fargo Active Cash or Chase Sapphire Preferred allow you to carry a balance during slow months, while the Amex Business Gold requires payment in full.

3. Consider the tax deduction angle

Real estate agents can deduct marketing, advertising, MLS fees, professional photography, staging, and client entertainment as business expenses. A business credit card keeps these expenses cleanly separated from personal spending, simplifying your Schedule C at tax time. Even the annual fee on a business card used primarily for real estate expenses is deductible, reducing the effective cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for real estate agents?
The Chase Sapphire Preferred is the best overall credit card for real estate agents because it earns 3x points on travel and dining -- the two categories where realtors spend the most between property showings, client meetings at restaurants, and travel for relocation clients. The 60,000-point welcome bonus provides immediate first-year value, and points transfer to airline and hotel partners for flexible redemption.
Do real estate agents need a business credit card?
Most real estate agents operate as independent contractors and benefit from a business credit card for expense separation and tax purposes. Business cards like the Ink Business Preferred keep marketing, advertising, and client entertainment expenses separate from personal spending, simplifying Schedule C deductions. However, agents early in their career with lower income may find personal cards easier to qualify for.
What is the best card for Zillow and real estate advertising?
The Chase Ink Business Preferred earns 3x points on online advertising including Zillow Premier Agent, social media ads, Google Ads, and other digital marketing platforms. For agents spending $1,000-$5,000+ monthly on advertising, this translates to 36,000-180,000+ bonus points annually. The Amex Business Gold may earn 4x on advertising if it falls in your top two spending categories.
What is the best card for client entertainment and dinners?
The Chase Sapphire Preferred earns 3x on all dining purchases, making it ideal for client entertainment dinners and coffee meetings. For agents who entertain clients frequently, this adds up quickly. The card also earns 3x on travel, covering property showing mileage. Points are transferable to travel partners, providing flexible redemption for both personal vacations and business travel.
What is the best no annual fee card for new real estate agents?
The Wells Fargo Active Cash is the best no-fee option for new agents because it earns 2% on all spending including marketing, professional photography, staging supplies, and client entertainment. New agents building their business should avoid high annual fees until commission income is consistent. The 2% flat rate ensures every expense earns rewards without worrying about category tracking.
Can real estate agents write off credit card annual fees?
Yes. Real estate agents who use a credit card primarily for business expenses can deduct the annual fee as a business expense on Schedule C. If the card is used for both personal and business spending, only the business percentage of the fee is deductible. Keep detailed records of business versus personal usage to support the deduction. Consult a tax professional for your specific situation.
What is the best card for luxury real estate agents?
The Capital One Venture X is the best card for luxury market agents because it provides Priority Pass lounge access for flying with relocation clients, a $300 annual travel credit, 2x miles on all purchases including high-end staging and client gifts, and travel insurance for expensive trips. The premium benefits project professionalism when entertaining high-net-worth clients.
Should realtors have multiple credit cards?
Many top-producing agents use a two-card strategy: a business card like the Ink Business Preferred for advertising and marketing expenses (3x on ads) paired with a personal card like the Chase Sapphire Preferred for client dinners and property showing travel (3x on dining and travel). This combination maximizes rewards across all major spending categories while keeping business and personal expenses clearly separated.
