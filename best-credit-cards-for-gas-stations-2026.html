How to Choose the Right Gas Card

1. Calculate your actual gas spending

Track your monthly fuel costs for 2-3 months. If you spend $200+/month on gas, a dedicated gas card with 4-5% back is worth it. At $150/month, a multi-category card like the Wells Fargo Autograph (3% gas plus dining, travel, streaming) may deliver more total value by earning bonuses across several categories you use regularly.

2. Check if your card works at all stations

The best gas cards earn bonus rewards at ANY gas station -- Shell, Exxon, Chevron, BP, independent stations, and even gas pumps at grocery stores. Avoid cards that only offer bonus rates at specific branded stations unless you exclusively fill up at that chain. Station-agnostic rewards give you the freedom to always choose the cheapest gas.

3. Watch for annual spending caps

Many gas cards cap their bonus rate after a certain annual threshold. The Costco Anywhere Visa caps gas rewards at $7,000/year (about $583/month), while the PenFed Platinum Rewards has no cap at all. If you drive for work or have a long commute, an uncapped card prevents your rewards from dropping mid-year.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for gas stations?
The Costco Anywhere Visa by Citi is the best overall gas card with 4% cash back at ALL gas stations nationwide (up to $7,000/year), plus 3% on restaurants and 2% at Costco -- all with no annual fee beyond your Costco membership. For drivers who spend $200-$400/month on gas, this translates to $96-$192 per year in fuel rewards alone.
Do gas cards work at all gas stations or just branded ones?
The best gas cards work at ALL gas stations, not just branded ones. The Costco Anywhere Visa, PenFed Platinum Rewards, and Citi Custom Cash all earn bonus rewards at any gas station regardless of brand. Some older or store-branded cards only work at specific chains, so always verify that your card earns bonuses at any station.
Does diesel fuel qualify for gas station rewards?
Yes, diesel fuel purchases at gas stations generally qualify for gas station bonus rewards. The rewards are based on the merchant category code (MCC) of the gas station, not the type of fuel purchased. Whether you buy regular, premium, or diesel, you earn the same bonus rate as long as the transaction codes as a gas station.
Do EV charging stations count for gas card rewards?
Most EV charging stations do not currently code as gas stations for credit card rewards. Public charging networks like ChargePoint, Electrify America, and Tesla Superchargers typically code as utilities or specialty services. Some cards are beginning to add EV charging as a bonus category, but this is not yet standard across the industry.
How much can I save with a gas credit card?
The average American spends $2,000-$4,000 per year on gas. With a 4% gas card, that translates to $80-$160 per year in savings. A 5% card earns $100-$200 annually. Even a 3% card generates $60-$120 per year. The savings increase proportionally with higher gas prices and longer commutes.
Is the Costco gas card worth it if I already have a membership?
If you already have a Costco membership, the Costco Anywhere Visa is essentially a free gas card with 4% back at all gas stations. There is no additional annual fee beyond your membership. The 4% rate is among the highest available for gas rewards, and you also earn 3% on dining and 2% at Costco. The main downside is that rewards are paid annually as a certificate.
Best no-fee gas card without Costco membership?
The Citi Custom Cash is the best no-fee gas card without requiring any membership. It automatically earns 5% on gas if fuel is your highest spending category each billing cycle. The PenFed Platinum Rewards also earns 5x on gas with no annual fee, though PenFed membership (free to join) is required.
Should I use a gas card or a flat-rate card for fuel?
A dedicated gas card almost always beats a flat-rate card for fuel purchases. Even a 3% gas card earns 50% more than a 2% flat-rate card on every fill-up. If you spend $250/month on gas, a 4% gas card earns $120/year vs $60/year with a 2% card -- double the rewards on fuel spending.
