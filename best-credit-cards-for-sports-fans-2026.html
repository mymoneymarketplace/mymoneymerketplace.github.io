How to Choose the Right Card as a Sports Fan

1. Identify your biggest sports expense

If game tickets are your top spend, the Citi Custom Cash's automatic 5% on entertainment is unbeatable. If sports bar dining dominates your game day budget, the Amex Gold's 4x on dining earns the most. If you stream multiple sports packages, the Capital One SavorOne's 3% on streaming adds up. Match your card to the category where you spend the most on sports.

2. Value presale access if you attend live events

Citi Entertainment and Amex Experiences both offer presale access to major sporting events, playoff games, and championship events. Getting tickets before the general public often means better seats at face value instead of paying resale markups. If you attend live games regularly, presale access can save hundreds per year on ticket costs alone.

3. Consider away game travel frequency

Fans who travel to away games should consider a travel rewards card like the Chase Sapphire Preferred. Earning 3x on flights, hotels, and dining while following your team on the road generates significant rewards. The 60,000-point welcome bonus alone could fund one or two away game trips including flights and hotels.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for buying sports tickets?
The Capital One SavorOne is the best card for sports tickets because it earns 3% on entertainment purchases, which is how most ticket purchases are coded. For heavy ticket buyers, the Citi Custom Cash earns 5% on your top spending category -- if entertainment is your highest spend, you automatically get 5% back on tickets up to $500 per billing cycle.
Do any credit cards offer presale access to sporting events?
Yes. Citi cards offer Citi Entertainment, which provides presale access to concerts, sporting events, and other live experiences. American Express cards offer similar presale access through Amex Experiences. Both programs frequently feature premium sporting events, playoff tickets, and championship games before they go on sale to the general public.
What is the best card for sports streaming subscriptions?
The Capital One SavorOne earns 3% on streaming services including ESPN+, YouTube TV, Peacock, DAZN, and other sports streaming platforms. The Blue Cash Preferred from Amex earns 6% on select U.S. streaming subscriptions. If you spend $100 or more per month on sports streaming, the 6% rate from the Blue Cash Preferred saves more despite its $95 annual fee.
Which card is best for sports bar spending on game days?
The Amex Gold earns 4x points on all dining including sports bars and restaurants, making it the highest-earning card for game day dining. The Chase Sapphire Preferred earns 3x on dining. For a no-fee option, the Capital One SavorOne earns 3% on dining with no annual fee. All three cards reward every burger, beer, and wing ordered during the game.
What is the best credit card for traveling to away games?
The Chase Sapphire Preferred is the best card for away game travel because it earns 3x on flights, hotels, and dining. Points transfer to major airline and hotel partners for maximum value on road trip travel. The 60,000-point welcome bonus is worth $750 or more toward away game travel expenses.
Do sports team credit cards offer good rewards?
Most team-branded credit cards offer lower rewards rates than general-purpose cards. They typically earn 1-2% with team-specific perks like discounts on merchandise or priority access to tickets. Unless you value the team branding and specific perks highly, a general rewards card with 2-3% back on relevant categories will earn significantly more over time.
What is the best no-fee card for sports fans?
The Capital One SavorOne is the best no-fee card for sports fans because it earns 3% on entertainment (tickets), 3% on dining (sports bars), and 3% on streaming (ESPN+, YouTube TV) -- the three main sports spending categories -- all with no annual fee. The Wells Fargo Active Cash is a simpler alternative with 2% on everything.
Can I earn rewards on team merchandise purchases?
Yes. Team merchandise purchased online from official team stores or retailers like Fanatics, Nike, or Dick's Sporting Goods earns standard rewards on most credit cards. The Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on all merchandise purchases. If you buy merchandise at the stadium during games, it may code as entertainment, earning 3% on the Capital One SavorOne.
