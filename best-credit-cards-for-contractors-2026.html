How to Choose the Right Card as a Contractor

1. Prioritize high credit limits over reward rates

Contractors need cards that can handle $10,000-$20,000 material purchases without hitting the ceiling. Business cards from Chase, Capital One, and Amex typically offer higher starting limits than personal cards. When applying, report your full gross business revenue to maximize your initial limit. A card with a slightly lower reward rate but a $30,000 limit is more useful than a 5% card with a $5,000 limit.

2. Use a flat-rate card for material purchases

Most building supply purchases at Home Depot, Lowes, and specialty supply houses do not fall into common bonus categories like gas or dining. A flat-rate card like the Capital One Spark Cash Plus ensures you earn 2% on every material purchase regardless of the merchant. This is simpler and often more rewarding than trying to fit supply runs into specific bonus categories.

3. Consider cash flow management features

Contractors often buy materials weeks or months before receiving client payment. Cards with 0% intro APR periods or extended billing cycles help bridge this gap without accruing interest. The Amex Business Gold provides additional time to pay, and many Chase business cards offer 0% intro APR for the first 12 months. These features act as short-term financing for project-based businesses.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for contractors?
The Ink Business Preferred is the best overall credit card for contractors because it offers a massive 100,000 point welcome bonus worth $1,250 or more, earns 3x on shipping and delivery charges common in construction, and provides purchase protection on materials. The $95 annual fee is easily offset by the bonus and ongoing rewards for contractors spending $5,000-$20,000 per project on materials and supplies.
What is the best credit card for buying building materials?
The Capital One Spark Cash Plus earns 2% on every purchase with no cap, making it ideal for large material purchases at Home Depot, Lowes, and specialty supply houses. For contractors who shop exclusively at Lowes, the Lowes Advantage Card offers 5% off every purchase or extended financing on large orders, which can save more on individual purchases.
Should contractors use store cards or regular credit cards?
Most contractors benefit more from a general business credit card than a store card. A card like the Spark Cash Plus earns 2% at every retailer, not just one store. However, the Lowes Advantage Card offering 5% off or extended financing can be valuable as a secondary card for contractors who make frequent Lowes purchases. The best strategy is pairing a general business card with one store card.
What credit limit do contractors need?
General contractors typically need credit limits of $15,000-$50,000 or more to cover material purchases for large projects without maxing out the card. Business cards generally offer higher limits than personal cards. Reporting your full gross business revenue on the application and maintaining a strong business credit profile helps secure higher limits.
Can a sole proprietor contractor get a business credit card?
Yes. Sole proprietor contractors qualify for business credit cards from all major issuers. You can apply using your legal name as the business name, your Social Security number, and your gross contracting revenue. You do not need an LLC, EIN, or formal business entity. Most issuers including Chase, American Express, and Capital One regularly approve sole proprietor applications from contractors.
What is the best no annual fee card for contractors?
The Ink Business Cash is the best no-fee business card for contractors. It earns 5% on office supplies, internet, and phone services, plus 2% on gas and restaurants with no annual fee. For contractors who want simplicity, the Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on everything including material purchases with zero annual fee.
Do contractor credit cards offer purchase protection?
Yes. Many business credit cards provide purchase protection that covers materials against damage or theft for 90-120 days after purchase. The Ink Business Preferred offers purchase protection up to $10,000 per claim and $50,000 per account, which is valuable for expensive materials stored on job sites.
How do contractors manage cash flow with credit cards?
Contractors can use credit cards strategically to bridge the gap between material purchases and client payments. By purchasing materials on a card with a 0% intro APR period or extended billing cycle, contractors effectively get 30-60 days of free financing. The Amex Business Gold provides extended payment terms, and many cards offer 0% intro APR for 12-15 months on new purchases.
