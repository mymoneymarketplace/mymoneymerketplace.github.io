How to Choose the Right Card as a Fitness Enthusiast

1. Identify your biggest fitness expense

If health food and organic groceries are your top spend, the Blue Cash Preferred at 6% on supermarkets provides the most value. If your gym membership is your largest single fitness expense, the Citi Custom Cash may earn 5% automatically. If your fitness spending is spread across many categories (gym, gear, supplements, food), a flat 2% card like the Wells Fargo Active Cash earns consistently everywhere.

2. Prioritize no-fee cards to protect your fitness budget

Fitness enthusiasts already allocate a significant portion of their budget to health and wellness. Adding a $95+ annual card fee on top of gym memberships, supplements, and gear cuts into your fitness fund. Most of our top picks carry no annual fee, ensuring every dollar of cash back goes toward your fitness goals rather than paying card fees.

3. Consider how gym memberships are coded

Gym memberships can code as recreation, health clubs, or general services depending on the gym chain. This means category-specific cards may or may not earn bonus rates on your gym. Before choosing a card based on a bonus category, check how your specific gym codes transactions. A flat-rate card eliminates this uncertainty entirely by earning the same rate regardless of merchant category codes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best credit card for gym memberships?
The Wells Fargo Active Cash is the best card for gym memberships because it earns 2% cash back on all gym payments regardless of how the transaction is coded. Gym memberships can code as recreation, health clubs, or general services depending on the gym, making flat-rate cards more reliable than category-specific cards. The Citi Custom Cash may earn 5% if your gym becomes your top monthly spending category.
Can I earn rewards on athletic gear and shoes?
Yes. Athletic gear from stores like Nike, lululemon, Dick's Sporting Goods, and REI earns standard rewards on any credit card. The Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on all athletic gear purchases. For online purchases, the Amazon Prime Rewards Visa earns 5% on Amazon athletic gear. The Citi Custom Cash may earn 5% if sporting goods stores are your top category.
What is the best card for buying supplements and protein?
If you buy supplements at grocery stores, the Blue Cash Preferred from Amex earns 6% on U.S. supermarket purchases. If you order online from supplement retailers, the Wells Fargo Active Cash earns a reliable 2% on everything. For Amazon supplement orders, the Amazon Prime Rewards Visa earns 5%. The best card depends on where you buy your supplements.
Do any credit cards offer gym or fitness perks?
No major credit cards offer direct gym membership discounts or fitness-specific perks as a standard benefit. Some premium Amex cards include wellness credits through hotel partners, and certain corporate card programs offer gym discounts. For most fitness enthusiasts, the best approach is maximizing cash back on fitness spending rather than seeking fitness-specific card perks.
What is the best card for health food and organic groceries?
The Blue Cash Preferred from Amex is the best card for health food because it earns 6% cash back at U.S. supermarkets including Whole Foods, Sprouts, Trader Joe's, and other health-focused grocers. If you spend $500 per month on organic groceries and health food, that is $30 per month or $360 per year in cash back from groceries alone.
What is the best no-fee card for fitness spending?
The Wells Fargo Active Cash is the best no-fee card for fitness enthusiasts because it earns 2% on everything -- gym memberships, athletic gear, supplements, health food, race fees, and fitness apps. The Capital One SavorOne is also a strong no-fee option with 3% on groceries (for health food) and 3% on streaming (for fitness apps).
Can I earn cash back on race entry fees?
Yes. Race entry fees for marathons, triathlons, obstacle course races, and other fitness events earn standard rewards on any credit card. These typically code as recreation or event tickets. The Wells Fargo Active Cash earns 2% on all race fees. The Capital One SavorOne may earn 3% if the fee codes as entertainment.
What is the best card for fitness app subscriptions?
The Capital One SavorOne earns 3% on streaming and subscription services, which includes fitness apps like Peloton, Strava Premium, MyFitnessPal, and Apple Fitness+. The Blue Cash Preferred from Amex earns 6% on select U.S. streaming subscriptions. Check whether your specific fitness app qualifies as a streaming subscription with your card issuer.
